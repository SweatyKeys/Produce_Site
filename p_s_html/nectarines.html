Nectarine Season

Nectarines can be available as early as middle of May, though the sweeter varieties aren't available till June. While California produces the most, you can find nectarines from Washington, Georgia and South Carolina.

Ripe Nectarine

Ripe nectarines have three indicators, being color, feel and smell. Look for a deep yellow color, as varieties can show a different color of red to their skin. If there is any hint of green, commonly seen near the stem end,the fruit needs a few days to ripen. A ready to eat nectarine should appear firm, though yield under gentle pressure. If too soft, the fruit is either bruised or overripe. If the fruit appears to be hard like a stone(tehehe), allow the fruit a few more days to ripen (though some claim to enjoy a crunchy nectarine). If the nectarine has no smell, it is highly suspect to being underripe, as ripe fruit can be fragrant and aromatic.
