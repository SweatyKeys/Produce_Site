Avocado Season

In 2002, California avocado growers aligned themselves with importers and organized a group called Haas Avocado Board, for growers and distributors to have a forum with the intent of coordinating national distribution under one roof. Considering the growing season for Californian avocados lasts from February till August, we see supplementation nation wide from Mexico, whose output of avocados eclipses California's 3:1 with their growing of avocados lasting nearly all year long. With that being said, the United States also supplements their demand for the fruit with avocados from Peru, Chile, Dominican Republic and New Zealand.

Ripe Avocados

Two indicators for a ripe avocado are color and feel. A ripe avocado will have dark skin, and a slight give to gentle pressure. It will feel soft, but not mushy, and for the love of all that is holy, do not firmly grip it, till it gives, because you are only bruising the delicious fruit inside. Bright green colored fruit, that feel rock hard will ripen in 4 to 5 days. "Breaking" avocados which have a darker skin , and that feel softer, but do not yield under gentle pressure, will take one to two days to ripen. To speed the ripen process, but the unripened fruit in a brown paper bag with a banana or apple.
