Cantaloupe Season

A majority of cantaloupes grown in the United States are produced in California, specifically the San Joaquin Valley. To keep up with the demand, growers plant seeds in March and in June. Those planted in March take 90-100 days to reach maturity while those planted in June take 75 days. Peak season for Cantaloupe is considered to be from June through August.

Ripe Cantaloupe

The indicators for a ripe cantaloupe are color, smell, feel, and texture.A ripe cantaloupe will have a cream, tan or yellow rind. A grey, green rind with flat webbing is a sign of unripe fruit, however this depends on the variety of cantaloupe. Check other indicators, or google the specific variety type (this website is for broader applications, sorry). Smell the blossom end of the melon (opposite of the stem side) where a ripe melon should smell florally sweet. When holding the melon, see that it feels heavier than it appears, and avoid those that are rock hard. A small amount of give blossom sign is a good sign as well. The webbing of a melon should appear raised in some parts and take notice of the stem site. A stem site that has a smooth, round belly button appearance is a good indicator, as opposed to rough or torn, lumpy, and still have nubs of stem attached that are signs of unripened fruit. Cantaloupes with dents, punctures or brown and black patches should be avoided as well.
