Watermelon Season

While grown all over the United States, the majority of melon's production occurs in Texas, Florida, Georgia and California. Limited quantities of watermelon are available, in May, as peak production starts in August and lasts till November.

Ripe Watermelon

The indicators for a ripe watermelon are appearance, feel, and sound. A ripe watermelon will have a pale yellow spot on the rind. When picking the melon up, it should feel heavier than it looks. One of the more common indicators though often times confused, is the sound the watermelon makes when flicking, or lightly knocking on it. The melon should have a hollow sound, as opposed to a heavier dense sound like a "thud."
