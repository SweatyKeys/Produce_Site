Pineapple Season

Pineapple is available year round, however peak season is from March through July.The top pineapple producing countries are Costa Rica, Brazil, Philippines, Thailand and Indonesia.

Ripe Pineapple

The three indicators for ripe pineapple are color, feel and smell.

A ripe pineapple sweet tasting pineapple will have a gold color, from the bottom to the crown. If it is gold on the bottom and green on top, flavor will likely be inconsistent. The higher and more gold present, the more sugar and likely hood the fruit will be sweet. When smelling the fruit, do so from the butt of the fruit. If the odor is sweet, the fruit is ripe, and ready to eat. If there isn't much of a smell, it is unlikely to get sweet, as pineapple don't tend to ripen much more after picking. If there is an odor that is more fermented smelling, or like vinegar, it is overripe and should be avoided. A ripe pineapple will also yield slightly when given a little pressure.
