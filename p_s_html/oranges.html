Oranges Season

Considering oranges is a relatively broad spectrum of citrus due to the different varieties commercially produced, navel oranges are possibly the most widely available. Navels are harvested from December through March.Pink fleshcara cara's are available December till Apriland the popular "juicing orange," velencias are available starting January till November.While technically a hybrid, however wildly popular, clementines are available November till January.

Ripe Oranges

There are two indicators for a ripe orange which are feel and smell. Due to difficult shipping conditions, picking the fruit from trees is a fine balance between not too soon, and not too ripe. This is due shipping fresh ripe oranges risk the chance of the fruit often being bruised or rot when reaching its destination. To find the best available orange, pick up fruit judging its size, compared to the weight of it in your hand. Heavier feeling fruit will be more likely the tastier choice. As well, smell the orange from where it blossoms (opposite of stem site)as oranges covered by wax will give off a citrus sweet aroma when ripe. Avoid any that resemble the smell of wine.
