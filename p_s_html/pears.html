Pears Season

In general, pear harvest season starts in fall and ends depending on variety, in middle of Winter or even all the way till mid Spring. Due to cooling methods to preserve fruit, you can find the most popular varieties all year round, however, the fruit won't be as good as it will be during harvest season. Bartlett pears are harvested in late August, early September through early February. D'anjou pears are available in late September though are most commonly seen available year round. Bosc pears harvest begins in the fall in Oregon and Washington, which lasts till the end of April.

Ripe Pears

Due to each pear variety being significantly different from each other, indicators for ripeness vary depending on the type of pear you choose. Bartlett pear ripeness is shown by it's skin color . Yellow-green bartletts will have a yellow-green skin, and slightly moist texture. For a more sweet and juicy flavor and texture, wait for the pear to turn to a golden yellow hue.D'anjou pear ripeness will appear green even when ripe. The best way to check for ripeness is to lightly press near the stem site using your thumb. If the pear gives slightly it is ready to eat!
