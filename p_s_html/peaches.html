Peach Season

While peaches can be seen as early as late Spring, they aren't in peak season until late June, and lasts till late August, early September.The seasons largely depend on where the supply is being shipped from. The biggest producer is California (which can be affected by drought), and other producing states include, Texas, Florida, Georgia, North and South Carolina, and Virginia.

Ripe Peaches

There are three indicators for a ripe peach being color, feel and smell.>Look for a deep golden hue behind the red blush of the fruit . Any green color near the stem, flat spots, or bruises should be avoided as well. There are a few signs that the feel of a peach can indicate. If the fruit feels hard as a rock, the peach will unlikely ever become ripe as it was picked too early. If it feels similar to a tennis ball, it is not ready to eat, however leave it for a few days, and it will ripen. A peach that yields lightly to gentle pressure are ready, and even those that are soft, are considered to be eaten right away. There is a thing as too soft, being a sign of bruising, which can happen even without being handled too much. they can be salvaged as edible, however messy and possibly made better for other uses such as a sauce or stirred in yogurt.
