Persimmon Season

Persimmons are in season from October till February. Hachiya and fuyu persimmons are the two varieties widely available in they United States, with fuyu making up nearly 80 percent of the market. A fuyu persimmon is small and squatty like a donut, and the hachiya persimmon has a similar shape of an acorn.

Ripe Persimmon

The fuyu persimmon is truly ripe, when the leafy top can easily peel off. Fuyus are sweet even when unripe, and only slightly soft. Hachiyas are known for being astringent, so its important to only eat them when they are ripe. A ripe hachiya will have a soft feel when holding the fruit, almost as if is filled with jelly. The best indicator for knowing its ready to eat, is when you can easily peel the leafy top off.
