Kiwi Season

Most commercial kiwifruit are grown in New Zealand. New Zealands seasons are different from the United States, as our winter months are considered their summer months. Kiwifruit growing season lasts about 240 days within a year. Harvest season begins during New Zealand Fall (March-May). This is supplemented by kiwifruit grown in California as well, which are harvested in United States Fall months (October-November).

Ripe Kiwi

The indicators for ripe kiwi are color, feel, and smell. Choose kiwi that have a tan, brown color, with no green. Kiwi that are ripe, have a citrus fragrance, and yields to gentle pressure. If a kiwi has a hard ,core and a tart taste, it is unripe. Kiwi's that don't fit the description for being ripe, can be set out for a day or two, or be left in a paper bag with a banana to speed the process.
