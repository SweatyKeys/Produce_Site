Grapefruit Season

Grapefruits are commonly grown in Texas, and Florida, although, parts of California are known to harvest the fruit as well. In parts of Texas where Winter is rather tame, the big citrus can be harvested close to year round. Peak season however is from October till June, with the first of Winter crop being texans favorite of the harvest. Florida sees similar peaks in their season, however it depends on the varietywhich includes Duncan, Red, and White Marsh grapefruit. For grapefruit in California, harvest is in late summer and fall, leading into peak season for Texas and Florida.

Ripe Grapefruit

Indicators for a ripe grapefruit include color, feel, and shape. Ripe fruit will be tinted red, the deeper the color the more flavor. Grapefruits should feel plump, smooth, and as you hold it in your hand, feel heavier than it looks. Think of it like a ball, if it is hollow, it will feel lighter, it is filled, it will be heavier to hold like a juicy grapefruit. As it's in your hand, check for soft spots, as this is an indication for rot. Ripe grapefruits will be slightly oval with flat tops and bottoms. Any round at the top (underripe), or oddly shaped (overripe) aren't prime fruit.
