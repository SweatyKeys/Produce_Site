Honeydew Season

Honeydews produced in the United States are mostly grown in California, and Arizona, though globally China leads as the biggest producer of melons in general, with Turkey following in second. Honeydews season in the United States goes from June through October.

Ripe Honeydew

The indicators for a ripe honeydew are appearance and color, feel, and smell.A ripe honeydew should have no signs of green, with a creamy, yellow or white color. The melon should not have any soft spots or discoloration from its overall skin tone. A ripe melon will have small ripples, and feel more velvety than smooth. When holding the honeydew in your hand, it should feel heavier than it looks. Lightly press near the blossom end (opposite of the stem site) which a ripe melon will have a small amount of give.
